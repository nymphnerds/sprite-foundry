# Subject Sheet — Kael Morrow

## Role
Ship engineer and salvage specialist aboard a merchant freighter. Practical, built for crawlspaces and hull work. Mid-30s, lean but broad-shouldered.

## Consumer
Star Freight (sci-fi space merchant RPG)

## Generation Target
48×48 pixel art sprite, 8 directions, HD-2D style

---

## Silhouette

Broader than Sera Vale. The half-coat and shoulder pad create an asymmetric upper-body mass that must read at 48px. The welding mask on top of the head adds a distinctive crown shape visible from front and side. Overall silhouette should read "mechanic in a heavy coat" not "soldier" or "mage."

**Silhouette priority (what must read at 48px):**
1. Half-coat shape (longer left side)
2. Shoulder pad bulk on right
3. Welding mask crown on head
4. Tool belt low on left hip

---

## Costume Landmarks (7)

| # | Landmark | Position | Notes |
|---|----------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Heavy half-coat | Torso, asymmetric hem | Dark charcoal-grey, longer on LEFT side (mid-thigh), shorter on RIGHT (waist-length). Work-worn. |
| 2 | Reinforced shoulder pad | RIGHT shoulder | Riveted metal plate, slightly lighter grey than coat. Must be visible from front and right-side views. |
| 3 | Undershirt | Chest, under coat | Dark olive-green, high collar, visible at neckline and where coat opens. |
| 4 | Tool belt | LOW on LEFT hip | Wide leather belt, sits lower than waist. 2-3 visible tool shapes (wrench silhouette, multi-tool). |
| 5 | Welding mask | TOP of head, pushed up | Rectangular mask flipped up onto forehead/top of head. Dark lens, lighter frame. Key front/back distinction marker. |
| 6 | Heavy boots | Feet | Thick-soled, reinforced toe caps, dark brown. Bulkier than Sera's mid-calf boots. |
| 7 | Work gloves | Hands | Thick fingerless gloves, dark leather with lighter palm patches. |

---

## Asymmetry Anchors (3)

| Anchor | Side | Why it matters |
|--------|------|----------------|
| Shoulder pad | RIGHT | Breaks left/right symmetry at shoulder level. Must track correctly through turnaround. |
| Tool belt | LEFT hip (low) | Breaks left/right symmetry at hip level. Opposite side from shoulder pad. |
| Half-coat hem | LEFT longer | The coat's asymmetric cut is the hardest consistency test. Left side hangs to mid-thigh, right side stops at waist. |

**Rotation expectation:**
- **Front:** Shoulder pad visible on viewer's left (character's right). Tool belt visible on viewer's right (character's left). Coat hem asymmetry visible.
- **Right-facing:** Shoulder pad prominent (near side). Short coat hem visible. Tool belt hidden or barely visible.
- **Back:** Shoulder pad visible on viewer's right. Coat drapes longer on viewer's right (character's left). Welding mask visible from behind on top of head.
- **Left-facing:** Tool belt prominent (near side). Long coat hem visible. Shoulder pad hidden or barely visible.

---

## Hair

Shaggy, medium-length, swept back. Dark auburn/reddish-brown. Not neat — some strands fall forward. More volume than Sera's tight bun. The hair mass changes silhouette shape between front (frames face) and back (falls behind welding mask).

**Hair pressure points:**
- Front: hair frames face, welding mask sits on top
- Side: hair mass visible behind ear, swept back
- Back: hair hangs below welding mask, visible volume
- Diagonals: hair + mask + shoulder pad all compete for silhouette space

## Must Stay True

1. Half-coat is ALWAYS asymmetric — longer on character's LEFT
2. Shoulder pad is ALWAYS on character's RIGHT
3. Tool belt is ALWAYS on character's LEFT hip, sitting LOW
4. Welding mask is ALWAYS on TOP of head, pushed UP (never down over face)
5. Hair has visible VOLUME — never slicked flat
6. Boots are BULKY — never slim
7. Single character — no doubles, no reflections, no turnaround sheet composition

---

## Forbidden Drift

- Cape or flowing fabric (the coat is heavy and structured, not flowing)
- Weapon in hand (tools only, holstered/belted)
- Helmet instead of welding mask (mask is pushed UP, not worn)
- Backpack or large pack (tool belt is the carry system)
- Clean/pristine appearance (work-worn, not polished)
- Symmetrical coat (the asymmetry is the point)
- Armor plating beyond the single shoulder pad
- Bright colors or glowing elements

---

## Front/Back Distinction Test

This is the key pressure the welding mask creates:

- **Front view:** Welding mask lens faces viewer, dark rectangular shape on forehead above face
- **Back view:** Welding mask frame visible from behind on top of head, hair falls below it
- **If these are indistinguishable, the character has failed the front/back test**

---

## Why This Subject

Kael Morrow pressures the pipeline on every axis Sera Vale did not:
- **Hair mass** that changes shape by direction (vs. Sera's minimal bun)
- **Asymmetric outerwear length** that must track through rotation (vs. Sera's symmetric vest)
- **Three asymmetry anchors** at different heights (vs. Sera's two at hip level)
- **Distinctive head accessory** that tests front/back distinction (vs. Sera's clean head profile)
- **Broader silhouette** that challenges 48px readability (vs. Sera's compact frame)

If the pipeline holds Kael Morrow's identity across 8 directions, it's a system. If it doesn't, we'll know exactly where it breaks.
